About Organ Donation

Organ donation is the process of giving an organ or a part of an organ for the purpose of transplantation into another person. Organs that can be donated include the heart, lungs, liver, kidneys, pancreas, and intestines. Tissue such as corneas, skin, and bone can also be donated.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Who can donate organs?

    Anyone can become an organ donor, regardless of age or medical history.

  • Will my body be disfigured if I donate organs?

    No. Organ donation does not disfigure the body, and an open casket funeral is still possible.

  • How do I become an organ donor?

    You can sign up to become an organ donor when you apply for or renew your driver's license, or you can register online.
